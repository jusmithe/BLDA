Chicago Salsa Scene

We've recieved many questions about the social dancing scene outside the club-- our monthly and special event mixers can only provide so much dancing. As such, many of our instructors and students regularily explore the salsa scene--below are some of the locales you may be interested in.

BLDA

Okay, so we just admitted we can only provide so much dancing, but don't forget to come to open salsa, Seven Ten on the first or second Wed of every month (always check our facebook page for the next event!) and any special dances we may have, also available on our facebook page at least a week in advance as well as on our listhost.

Dance Studios

Chicago's dance studios are great locations to further improve your dancing skills. These locations not only offer advanced level classes, but also additional opportunities to perform, and regular mixers to practice your dancing.

Latin Street Dancing:
Website: http://www.laboriqua.com/
Address: 1335 W. Lake St, Chicago, IL
Fun facts: UofC and BLDA alumna, Stephanie Wolfson, teaches and directs at LSD!

Latin Rhythms:
Website: http://www.latinrhythmsdance.com/
Address: 210 N. Racine, Chicago, IL
Fun facts: Latin Rhythms is home to Bachata Legacy and LR Dance Company. They host great socials and have a gorgeous space!

Mixed Motion Art:
Website: http://www.mixedmotionart.com/
Address: 2355 N. Milwaukee, Chicago, IL
Fun facts: MMA teaches only on2, a.k.a. mambo, and the owners Laura and Del offer great technical instruction focusing on details.

Salsa Nights in Chicago

Chicago has a vibrant salsa/bachata nightlife! Below are the two main organizers of salsa events, they will often have deals for people who RSVP in advance, or something similar. Often these salsa nights will also offer a complimentary lesson before the DJ begins spinning. General rule: get there at 10:30pm.

Tuesday: Alhambra [1240 W Randolph St.] Cover is $5 if you RSVP on facebook! An older crowd of pretty good dancers, especially hopping when there's a performance. Lesson at 7:30. Doors open at 7pm, goes until 2am. Easy access from Green Line Morgan stop.21+.

Tuesday: Dolphin [2200 N Ashland Ave.] Cover is $5. Usually not too crowded unless there's a performance or competition. Club feel. As of May 2014, they are trying out a live bachata band that does horrible Royce covers, but the band is only on for an hour.21+.

Wednesday: Footrise at Candela [2451 N. Milwaukee Ave.] Cover is 7$, dancing until 1. Not crowded and a nice place with a spacious dance floor. El Caobo is the DJ. Run by MMA. Easy access from Logan Square Blue Line stop.21+.

Wednesday: Lalo's on Maxwell [733 W Maxwell St.] Cover is free! Usually super packed to the point of not being able to dance without getting stepped on, but there are some great dancers there every week and delicious margaritas.21+.

Thursday: Castle [632 N. Dearborn] Cover is 7$ (Free with LSD's VIS Card). Often has a surplus of leads, but it remains the only club which two seperate floors for latin dancing--one for salsa/chacha and another for bachata/merengue/cumbia. 21+

Thursday: Patron's [316 W Erie St.] No cover! This place is almost always super popular, with great dancers and fantastic passionfruit margaritas. 21+

Friday: Dylan's [118 S Clinton St.] Cover is $5-10. Usually a smaller, older crowd but on nights when nothing else is happening or there's a good band, it can get pretty packed with great dancers. 21+

Fridays and Saturdays: Latin Rhythms [210 N. Racine] has socials every other week or so. 5$ with membership, 10$ without. Always a popular event; check their website for details. All ages!

Sundays: Cubby Bear [1059 W Addison St.] Cover is $10 (Free with LSD's VIS Card). A very well attended salsa night, often has live music or competitions. 21+

Latin Street Dancing:
Website: http://www.laboriqua.com/
By signing up for their classes, you recieve a VIS card which gets you into their salsa events without a cover charge.

4 Fantastic Events:
Website: http://www.4fantasticevents.com/
Cover for their events is 10$, but drops down to 5$ with a RSVP (Facebook or email) and before 10:30pm. They have events nearly every night of the week, but the big one is Alhambra Palace on Tuesdays.
